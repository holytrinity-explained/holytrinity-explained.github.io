Most Holy Trinity Explained

Published on July 31, 2012

TThe Most Holy Trinity is a divine mystery that reveals the nature of the one true and living God. Though He is one in essence, He exists eternally in three distinct persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. This doctrine was first formally articulated by Tertullian, one of the early Church Fathers. It is a reality that transcends material understanding and must be approached through faith and divine revelation.

The Father

The Father is the first person of the Most Holy Trinity. He is the Creator of all things, both visible and invisible, and is recognized by Jesus Christ, the incarnate Son, as God and source of all. Together with the Son and the Spirit, the Father is fully God, yet He is distinct in personhood.

He is described in Scripture as the Savior of Israel and of all humanity. He created everything good, and gave humankind the free will to choose between good and evil. Though we are free to choose, God calls us to walk in goodness and righteousness.

Scripture references: Genesis 1:1, Genesis 1:26, 1 Corinthians 8:6, Isaiah 43:11, Deuteronomy 30:19

The Son

The Son is the second person of the Most Holy Trinity — Jesus Christ, our Lord. He is the incarnate Word of God. The divine nature of God includes His eternal Word; without it, God would not be complete. Through this Word — who became flesh — all things were created. Jesus is of the same essence as the Father. He is both God and from God, sent into the world to reveal the Father and to reconcile humanity to Him. Jesus declared Himself as "the way, the truth, and the life." He did nothing of His own accord but acted in perfect unity with the Father's will.

Born in Bethlehem, Jesus fulfilled the prophecy of Isaiah. He referred to Himself as the Good Shepherd, and after His resurrection, Thomas rightly proclaimed to Him, "My Lord and my God," a declaration Jesus did not rebuke, but affirmed with a blessing for those who believe without seeing.

Scripture references: John 1:1–3, John 10:30, John 13:13, John 17:3, 1 Timothy 2:5, John 14:6, John 5:30, Micah 5:2, John 10:11, John 20:28–29

The Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit is the third person of the Most Holy Trinity. He proceeds from the Father and the Son, and together with them, is glorified and worshipped as God. Scripture tells us that "God is Spirit" and that He is holy. The Holy Spirit inspired the prophets and anointed them for their mission.

Jesus commanded His disciples to baptize all nations "in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit," affirming the Trinitarian nature of God — one essence, three persons, eternally united in love and purpose.

Scripture references: 2 Corinthians 3:17, Isaiah 61:1, Matthew 28:19

Conclusion

The doctrine of the Most Holy Trinity expresses the mystery of one God in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. They are coequal, coeternal, and consubstantial — meaning of the same divine essence. The Father is the source of all; the Son is His eternal Word made flesh; and the Holy Spirit is the living presence of God, proceeding from the Father and the Son. To believe in the Trinity is to embrace the fullness of divine revelation — a God who is unity, diversity, and perfect love.
